Trinity-CAM (GPT) - IT Carve-out Charter

Johnson Controls International (JCI) is divesting the Air conditioning business to Robert Bosch GmbH. Change Request 1 records the approved JCI TSA extension through 31 Jul 2027. This does not change programme progress, milestone dates, or GoLive; it gives Infosys more merger-zone build-up time while users continue to work in the legacy JCI environment and therefore reduces pressure on Bosch.

Delivery model: Seller IT -> Merger Zone -> Buyer IT. The merger zone is a temporary operating bridge built and run by Infosys so that user, application, data, and service migrations can be sequenced without forcing direct cutover from JCI into Bosch.
TSA position: JCI has approved a TSA extension through 31 July 2027 because the merger zone is not yet ready and existing-user migration cannot start earlier. Users remain on the legacy JCI environment during this buffer period while Infosys continues the merger-zone build, and the overall programme milestones remain unchanged.

Objectives

  • Establish a controlled separation path for 12,000 users currently operating on JCI infrastructure across 48 sites.
  • Prepare and migrate 1,800+ applications, including the major SAP estate, through the merger zone into Bosch-ready landing patterns.
  • Reach QG4 with completed user migration, stable application hosting, validated security controls, and no unresolved blocking defects.
  • Execute GoLive on 01.01.2028 and close all remaining TSA dependencies during post-GoLive stabilisation, using the approved seller-side buffer to remove avoidable pre-GoLive pressure on Bosch.
  • Maintain business continuity for manufacturing, supply chain, finance, workplace, and service operations throughout build, migration, and cutover.
  • Keep governance, cost, and risk control aligned across Bosch, JCI, KPMG, and Infosys for the full programme duration.
  • Hand over a stable operating model to Bosch by 01.04.2028 after 90 days of hypercare.
Opportunity in register: R023 - Phase 1 architecture and application triage identify a meaningful subset of applications and infrastructure services that can be landed in a more modern, lower-cost pattern inside the merger zone without delaying GoLive.

Assumptions and Constraints

  • Infosys remains the primary delivery partner for merger-zone setup, operation, and migration execution.
  • JCI continues to provide timely source-system access, SME availability, and TSA support through the approved extension date of 31 Jul 2027 while users remain on the legacy environment.
  • Major application and SAP dependency mapping reaches sufficient fidelity during early discovery to maintain the current phase plan.
  • Buyer-side landing patterns remain stable enough to avoid repeated redesign of merger-zone controls.
  • 01.01.2028 remains a hard business target and cannot absorb broad late-phase scope growth.
  • Cross-border data transfer and local labour-law constraints may force country-specific sequencing decisions.
  • Budget baseline and non-labour contingency funding remain subject to QG1 approval.
  • Post-GoLive work is limited to stabilisation, TSA exit, and Bosch handover, not new transformation scope.
