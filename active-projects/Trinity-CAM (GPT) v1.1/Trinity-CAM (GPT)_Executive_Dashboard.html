Project Overview

Trinity-CAM (GPT) is the IT carve-out programme for the sale of the air conditioning business from JCI to Bosch. Change Request 1 updates the baseline to reflect JCI approval of a TSA extension through 31 Jul 2027 so users can continue to work in the legacy JCI environment while Infosys completes merger-zone build-up.

The delivery model remains a true integration carve-out: JCI IT services remain the source estate, Infosys builds and operates the merger zone as the temporary landing environment, and Bosch accepts the stabilised operating model after GoLive and hypercare. Programme progress and milestone dates remain unchanged; the extension simply adds buffer and removes near-term pressure from Bosch.

The largest execution pressure remains on SAP carve-out complexity, merger-zone platform readiness, and final QG4 evidence closure ahead of the hard GoLive target on 01 Jan 2028, but the approved TSA buffer materially improves the run-up to migration start.

Critical Path and Guiding Principles
Merger Zone Platform
Hosting, identity, and network stack must be stable before broad migration starts.
Operational monitoring and DR controls need proof before test exit.
Any platform delay compresses all downstream migration windows.
SAP Critical Path
System copy, role redesign, and interface rewiring drive the highest schedule exposure.
Mock cutovers and rollback evidence must close before QG4.
SAP remains the single most material path to GoLive risk.
User and Site Migration
All four user waves and all 48 sites must complete before final gate approval.
Local readiness, legal consultation, and support coverage affect wave throughput.
Post-GoLive work is stabilisation only, not deferred migration.
Programme Principles
GoLive on 01 Jan 2028 remains the hard business commitment.
No new transformation scope enters after QG4.
The approved TSA extension reduces pressure before migration start; formal TSA exit and Bosch handover still complete during hypercare, not later.
