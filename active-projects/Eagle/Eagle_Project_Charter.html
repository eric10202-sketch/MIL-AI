Eagle Project Charter

Security Camera Business IT Carve-Out

Status: Draft
Date: 4 April 2026

1. Project Purpose & Business Case

Objective: Establish an independent IT operating environment for the Buyer to operate the Security Camera Business with standalone infrastructure, enabling strategic flexibility to modernize applications and pursue cloud-native architecture post-carve-out.

Strategic Rationale: Stand-alone carve-out (vs. integration into Buyer's legacy environment) provides: (1) separate data boundaries for GL posting independence; (2) standalone infrastructure enabling future cloud migration; (3) operational independence post-TSA exit; (4) vendor negotiation leverage for licensing and support.

Business Scope: 3 manufacturing sites (Singapore), 300 IT users, 15+ applications (SAP MM, legacy WMS, security systems, monitoring tools). Carve-out excludes Buyer's legacy business operations but includes shared ISV relationships subject to change-of-control renegotiation.

2. Carve-Out Model & Key Characteristics

Stand Alone Model No integration zone; separate compute, storage, backup, DR; standalone infrastructure investment (EUR 150–200K CAPEX); data segregation complete; GL posting independent.
TSA Duration 90 days post-Go-Live (Nov 8, 2026 – Feb 6, 2027) providing hypercare, infrastructure operations, 24/7 support, and capability transfer to Buyer IT.

4. Scope

In Scope

  • Standalone infrastructure (compute, storage, backup, DR)
  • SAP MM migration & dual-run data separation
  • Legacy WMS cloud-native consolidation
  • 12 application integration touchpoints (API layer)
  • ISV licensing re-negotiation
  • 90-day TSA-supported hypercare
  • Change management & training (300 users)
  • Security & PDPA compliance framework

Out of Scope

  • Integration into Buyer's legacy infrastructure
  • Buyer's non-carve-out business systems
  • Seller's post-TSA infrastructure operations
  • Organizational restructuring/staffing changes
  • Application development/enhancement work
  • Extended TSA beyond Feb 6, 2027 (except contingency)

7. Risks & Mitigation Strategy

Top 3 Threats Requiring Executive Oversight:

  • Phase 3 UAT Compression (27 days for 300 users × 15 apps): Mitigation — Front-load Phase 2 UAT by Jul 1; automate 60% of test scripts; deploy risk-based testing on critical path (SAP MM, GL, inventory).
  • SAP Data Separation & GL Posting Integrity: Mitigation — Complete data lineage audit by May 31; prototype ETL cleansing by Jul 15; establish dual-run reconciliation procedures by Sep 15; assign dedicated reconciliation owner.
  • Manufacturing Production Cutover During Q4 Peak: Mitigation — Finalize parallel-run procedures by Sep 30; establish 24/7 production support team; limit batch processing changes to Thu-Sat windows only during hypercare (Nov 8 – Feb 6).

8. Success Criteria & Handover

  • Production Go-Live Nov 7, 2026 (on schedule)
  • UAT defect escape rate < 5% (critical path only)
  • Hypercare SLA: 8-hour MTTR; <= 5 Priority-1 incidents/week post-GoLive
  • Data reconciliation accuracy > 99% by Dec 31, 2026
  • 90-day TSA hypercare delivered within budget (EUR 180–220K)
  • Buyer IT capability transfer complete; Seller support exit by Feb 6, 2027
  • Post-GoLive: Standalone operations documented; runbooks finalized; incident response procedures established
