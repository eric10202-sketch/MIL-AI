Project Duration 01 Apr 2026 – 30 Oct 2026 (7 months)
Scope 2 India Sites · 70 Users · 17 Apps · No ERP · No TSA

Budget Baseline EUR 554K (Labour) + CAPEX TBC – to be approved at QG0

Project Overview

Objective: Execute the carve-out of the Bosch BGSW AI Business into a newly formed 50/50 Joint Venture (JV) with Tata. Bosch retains leadership control of the JV, making this a non-antitrust-relevant transaction. The project separates AI-specific IT infrastructure, 17 applications, and 70 users across 2 India sites from BGSW's core systems by 1 July 2026 (Day 1 GoLive).

Strategic Context: Because Bosch maintains leadership control of the JV, the post-GoLive entity operates effectively as a Bosch-governed company (50% Tata shareholding). This simplifies the separation: no Merger Zone is required, no TSA is needed post-GoLive, and IT continuity risks are significantly reduced. Escalation paths remain via BGSW governance channels.

Sponsor Customer (Buyer): Tata (50% shareholder; Bosch holds leadership control).

Sponsor Contractor (Seller): Robert Bosch India (BGSW) as the carver entity.

Programme Manager: Riyaz Ahmed Syed Ahmed (BD/MIL-PSM4)

Scope & Model

In Scope

  • 17 AI applications (all in single migration wave)
  • 70 IT users across 2 India sites
  • ~70 client devices (reimaging to JV standard image)
  • M365 and Azure JV tenant (<70 mailboxes)
  • JV Active Directory forest (new, separate from BGSW)
  • Network setup across 2 India JV sites
  • Security and IAM platform for JV entity
  • JV legal entity setup (India; 50/50 Bosch-Tata)
  • HR IT mapping for 70 AI business users

Out of Scope

  • ERP systems (no SAP in scope)
  • BGSW core applications (non-AI business)
  • TSA services post-GoLive (not required)
  • International geographies (India only)
  • Manufacturing / OT systems
  • Antitrust or regulatory filings (not required)
  • Bosch Group shared services (retained by Bosch)
Carve-Out Model: Combination (Bosch-led JV)
Structure: 50/50 Bosch–Tata JV with Bosch holding leadership/operational control. Post-GoLive, the JV operates effectively as a Bosch-governed entity. No TSA Required: Because Bosch leads the JV, BGSW IT services are accessible via normal governance channels — no formal TSA contract is needed. Antitrust: Not applicable; Bosch leadership control means no merger control filing is required.
Key simplification vs. typical carve-outs: Bosch's JV leadership control eliminates the need for a Merger Zone, a TSA service catalogue, and antitrust regulatory filings. The separation is significantly simpler than a full Stand Alone model.

Key Success Criteria

  • Day 1 GoLive (1 Jul 2026): All 70 IT users and all 17 AI applications fully operational in the independent JV environment.
  • JV IT Independence: JV operates under Bosch leadership with its own AD forest, M365 tenant, and network; zero dependency on BGSW production systems.
  • Data Separation: All AI business data migrated to JV environment without data loss or compliance breach.
  • User Acceptance: UAT sign-off by 24 Jun 2026; hypercare closes 25 Sep 2026 without critical open issues.
  • No TSA Needed: JV self-sufficient from Day 1; BGSW accessible only via standard Bosch governance channels (not contractual TSA).
  • Budget Compliance: Project delivers within EUR 554K labour budget + approved CAPEX; no uncontrolled overrun.
  • Programme Closed by 30 Oct 2026 with QG5 passed and lessons learned documented.
