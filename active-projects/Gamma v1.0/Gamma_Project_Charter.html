Executive Summary

Gamma establishes the formal mandate for separating the Bosch Cloud business from Robert Bosch China and standing up the Day 1 operating environment for a new 50/50 JV with Alibaba. The programme scope covers 5 sites, 250 IT users, and 20 applications, with no SAP in scope and a strong emphasis on infrastructure, identity, service management, and operational readiness. The project is materially shaped by unresolved antitrust due diligence, high confidentiality, and a limited aware stakeholder group, so the delivery model relies on phased disclosure, legal dependency control, and tightly governed approvals.

The baseline plan targets GoLive on 01 February 2027, followed by a 90-day stabilization window and formal closure by 31 May 2027. Upstream baselines already created for Gamma are the schedule, risk register, and cost plan, and this charter reconciles to those current deliverables only.

Scope And Objectives

In Scope

  • Infrastructure separation and target environment setup for the new JV.
  • Identity, access, endpoint, service management, monitoring, and support readiness.
  • Separation and transition of approximately 20 applications.
  • Transition planning for 5 sites and 250 users.
  • Controlled interim service continuity where seller support is still required.
  • GoLive readiness, cutover, 90-day hypercare, and operational handover.

Out Of Scope

  • SAP separation or ERP transformation.
  • Manufacturing or OT scope.
  • Broad Day 2 transformation beyond the Day 1 JV operating baseline.
  • Post-closure strategic changes not required for Day 1 readiness.
  • Non-approved stakeholder disclosure outside the legal clean-team model.

Governance And Delivery Model

Governance Structure

  • Sponsor customer: Alibaba
  • Sponsor contractor: Robert Bosch China
  • PMO and methodology lead: EY
  • Joint steering decisions aligned to the 50/50 JV governance path.
  • Restricted clean-team model until legal allows broader disclosure.

Operating Model Note

Gamma uses the Combination carve-out model. During transition, interim service continuity can remain in place where required, but the target state is a jointly managed JV environment rather than a long-term merger-zone platform. This matters because legal, security, and decision-right design must support shared management without leaving Bosch China dependencies unresolved at Day 1.

Assumptions, Dependencies, And Success Criteria

Key Assumptions And Dependencies

  • Legal provides timely guidance on antitrust due diligence and permitted disclosure scope.
  • The limited clean-team model can be expanded in phases without causing rework to the operating baseline.
  • Shared Bosch China infrastructure dependencies are identified before Phase 2 build materially advances.
  • Third-party approvals for network, certificates, licences, and contract novation are secured before QG4.
  • Seller support remains available where required until the JV steady-state model is accepted.

Success Criteria

  • QG0 to QG5 milestones are passed on the approved baseline dates.
  • GoLive on 01 February 2027 occurs without critical service-continuity failure.
  • All 5 sites, 250 users, and 20 applications operate in the JV environment on Day 1.
  • No critical security or data-segregation issue remains open at QG4 or GoLive.
  • Hypercare completes in 90 days and the project closes on 31 May 2027.
