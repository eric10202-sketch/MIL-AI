## OVERVIEW
This communications plan defines the cadence, format, content, and distribution of project updates to ensure alignment with key stakeholders throughout the 24-month engagement.

---

## STAKEHOLDER GROUPS & COMMUNICATION STRATEGY

### 1️⃣ STEERING COMMITTEE (Executive Leadership)

**Stakeholders:** Bosch CIO, JCI EVP, Bosch CFO, Finance, Legal, Procurement, External Audit

**Frequency:** Monthly (in-person or hybrid)

**Format & Content:**
- **Meeting:** 2-hour formal session with voting on quality gates
- **Agenda:**
  - Executive summary (project health, budget, schedule variance)
  - Risk register review (top 5 risks, traffic light status)
  - Open points list (critical items requiring escalation/decision)
  - Phase deliverables status (% complete, blockers)
  - Financial tracking (actuals vs. budget)
  - Upcoming milestones and dependencies
  
**Key Documents Provided Before Meeting:**
- 1-page Steering Committee Brief (executive snapshot)
- Detailed risk register (CSV, sorted by rating)
- Open points list (priority items only)
- Updated schedule (critical path highlighted)

**Success Metrics:**
- All agenda items decided within meeting
- <5% of decisions escalated to executive level
- 95%+ attendance rate

**Owner:** IT Project Manager + KPMG Lead

---

### 2️⃣ EXECUTIVE LEADERSHIP (C-Suite)

**Stakeholders:** Bosch CEO/CFO, JCI President/CFO, Board members (investor, legal, audit)

**Frequency:** Quarterly (or on-demand for escalations)

**Format & Content:**
- **Executive Status Report (3–5 pages):**
  - Overall health (RAG status)
  - Key metrics: schedule variance, budget burn, critical milestones
  - Top 3 risks and mitigation actions
  - Major decisions required in next 90 days
  - Financial impact & cost forecast
  - Day 1 readiness assessment (Phase 5 onwards)

**Escalation Triggers:**
- Schedule variance >10% on critical path
- Budget variance >15%
- Any "Red" risk rating
- SPA/APA legal delays
- Vendor contract change-of-control blocks
- TSA scope disputes

**Distribution Channel:** Email + confidential board portal

**Owner:** IT PM + KPMG Partner

---

### 3️⃣ WORKSTREAM LEADS (IT Team Managers)
*IT Infrastructure, ERP/SAP, Applications, HR IT, Security, IT Org, Procurement, OT/Production IT*

**Stakeholders:** ~25 WS leads (Bosch + JCI + KPMG), Regional IT Directors

**Frequency:** Weekly (Tue 10am UTC — synchronized across AP/AM/EMEA)

**Format & Content:**
- **Weekly Workstream Sync (60 min):**
  - Status of in-flight tasks in each WS (% complete)
  - Blockers & dependencies on other WS
  - Resource allocation & capacity
  - Risk/issue escalation (items for Steering Committee)
  - Next week's priorities
  
- **Weekly Status Report (email):
  - Summary of deliverables completed
  - On-track / at-risk items
  - Next week forecast
  - Any blockers requiring IT PM intervention

**Key Tools:** MS Project schedule (updated weekly), OPL (open points list in Jira), Risk Register (shared Excel/CSV)

**Success Metrics:**
- 90%+ attendance at weekly syncs
- OPL updated within 24 hours of status change
- <3 days to unblock critical dependencies
- Risk register escalations <5 per week (average)

**Owner:** IT PM (chairs weekly sync)

---

### 4️⃣ PROJECT DELIVERY TEAMS (Individual Contributors)

**Stakeholders:** 100+ team members (Bosch, JCI, KPMG) — architects, engineers, admins, testers, business analysts

**Frequency:** Weekly + bi-weekly deep-dives per workstream

**Format & Content:**
- **Weekly All-Hands (30 min, Friday afternoon UTC):**
  - Wins of the week (celebrate progress)
  - Key milestones achieved
  - Upcoming critical tasks
  - Reminder: frozen zone rules, communication protocols
  - Q&A from team
  
- **Bi-Weekly Workstream Deep-Dive (90 min, per WS):**
  - Technical task reviews
  - Design walkthroughs
  - Knowledge sharing
  - Individual contributor blockers resolved
  
- **PMO SharePoint Site (always-on):**
  - Project calendar & milestones
  - Weekly status reports
  - Decision log
  - Architecture diagrams
  - Policy & procedures (frozen zone, change control, escalation)

**Communication Channels:**
- Email: Weekly all-hands summary + week-ahead forecast
- Slack/Teams: Real-time collaboration; dedicated #trinity-updates channel
- SharePoint: Central hub for docs, decisions, schedules
- In-person: Monthly regional townhalls (when co-located)

**Success Metrics:**
- >80% engagement in all-hands
- Bi-weekly attendance >90%
- Anonymous pulse survey: >70% understand project goals & their role
- <5 surprises at Steering Committee (everything escalated in advance)

**Owner:** PMO + Workstream Leads

### 5️⃣ EXTERNAL STAKEHOLDERS (Vendors, TSA Providers, Regulators)

**Stakeholders:** WAN providers, co-locators, SAP, M365, HR system vendors; government (tax, data protection)

**Frequency:** Quarterly + ad-hoc as needed

**Format & Content:**
- **Vendor Status Check (email or call, 30 min):**
  - Order status & delivery timeline
  - Change of control notifications
  - Contract amendments/license transfers

- **Regulatory Compliance Updates (quarterly):**

  - Change log for tax/customs (Brazil, China, India)

  - Handover training completion
  - Hypercare resource availability

**Owner:** Procurement + IT PM (vendor management)

### 6️⃣ BOARD MEMBERS / INVESTORS (Quarterly Review)

**Stakeholders:** Bosch Supervisory Board, JCI Board, External Investors (if applicable)

**Frequency:** Quarterly (with annual deep-dive at Q4)

**Format & Content:**

## CRISIS COMMUNICATION PROTOCOL

### Red Flag Scenarios & Response

| Scenario | Impact | Notification | Timeline | Response |
|----------|--------|---|---|---|
| **Schedule Delay >2 weeks on critical path** | Day 1 jeopardy | IT PM → Steering → Exec | 24 hours | Escalation + mitigation plan within 48 hours |
| **Budget overrun >20%** | CFO impact; board visibility | IT PM → CFO → Board | 24 hours | Revised forecast + cost reduction plan within 1 week |
| **Vendor contract change-of-control block** | Potential go-live blocker | IT PM → Procurement → Steering | 4 hours | Legal escalation; alternative vendor evaluation |
| **Data separation error discovered (pre-cutover)** | Compliance risk; Day 1 delay | Security Officer → CIO → Steering | 2 hours | Incident response; audit + remediation plan within 24 hours |
| **Key vendor outage (pre-cutover testing)** | Hypercare capacity risk | IT PM → Vendor → Steering | 2 hours | Workaround activation; 3-day recovery plan |
| **SPA/APA signing delayed (>2 weeks)** | Closing date jeopardy | Legal → Executive → Board | 1 hour | Impact assessment; go-live delay options (staggered vs. delay) |
| **Hypercare staffing shortfall (post-Day 1)** | Operational risk; service impact | IT Ops → IT PM → Executive | 24 hours | Temp staffing + escalation protocol activation |

### Communication Cadence During Crisis
- **Hour 0:** IT PM notifies Direct Manager + Steering Chair
- **Hour 2:** Status update to all Steering Committee members
- **Hour 4:** Stakeholder call (Steering + relevant WS leads + Exec)
- **Day 1:** Written mitigation/recovery plan to Steering
- **Daily:** Updates until resolution (5-day rule: all crises should trend toward resolution or escalate)

## KEY MESSAGES BY PHASE

### Phase 1 (Initialization)
- **Message:** "Team mobilized. Foundations being set. Governance in place. On track for Phase 1 QG."
- **Cadence:** Weekly (execution focus)
- **Tone:** Professional, collaborative, detail-oriented

### Phase 2 (Concept)
- **Message:** "Architecture designed. Migration strategy finalized. App wave plan locked. Ready for Development."
- **Cadence:** Weekly + biweekly deep-dives
- **Tone:** Technical confidence, risk awareness

### Phase 3 (Development)
- **Message:** "Infrastructure being built. 3 regional DCs coming online. Merger Zone taking shape."
- **Cadence:** Weekly + real-time risk escalation (if issues)
- **Tone:** Build momentum, highlight wins

### Phase 4 (Implementation)
- **Message:** "Migrations underway. Wave-based approach working. Performance strong. Day 1 readiness on track."
- **Cadence:** Daily standup (for Day-1-critical tasks); weekly for rest of WS
- **Tone:** Tactical, urgent, focused on Day 1

### Phase 5 (Go-Live)
- **Message:** "Day 1 cutover successful. Independent operations live. TSA activated. Hypercare in effect."
- **Cadence:** Real-time (incident-driven) + daily summary
- **Tone:** Calm, transparent, issue-resolution focused

### Phase 6 (Stabilization)
- **Message:** "Hypercare underway. Systems stable. TSA exit on track. Project wrapping up."
- **Cadence:** Weekly (incidents) + bi-weekly review
- **Tone:** Resolution-focused, lessons-learned capture

## TONE & MESSAGING GUIDE

### For Executives & Board
- **Tone:** Professional, decisive, risk-aware
- **Focus:** Business impact, budget, schedule, critical decisions
- **Avoid:** Technical jargon, excessive detail, uncertainty
- **Example:** "Project on track. Budget within 5% of forecast. One mitigation plan in progress (vendor lead time). Steering decision required on TSA scope by Apr 30."

### For IT Teams
- **Tone:** Collaborative, technical, action-oriented
- **Focus:** Task progress, blockers, technical decisions, learning
- **Avoid:** Politics, vague commitments, scope creep
- **Example:** "WAN RFP issued; lead time confirmed 5 months. AD design review Thu 2pm. File share architecture decision needed from infrastructure team by Wed."

### For All Staff
- **Tone:** Transparent, inclusive, celebratory (wins)
- **Focus:** Progress, milestones, "why" we're doing this, individual role clarity
- **Avoid:** Bad news without mitigation plan, blame, uncertainty
- **Example:** "Last week: 15 high-priority tasks completed. WAN RFP issued (critical path item). Next: architecture reviews. Your role: provide feedback on design by Wed."
