Project Falcon

BGSW AI Business Carve-Out into 50/50 JV | Combination Model (Carver + JV)

Project Duration 01 May 2026 – 29 Jan 2027 (9 months)
Scope 3 India Sites | <100 Users | ~20 Apps
Carve-Out Model Combination (Carver + JV)
Budget EUR 1.05M (Labour) | TBC (CAPEX)

Project Overview

Objective: Execute the carve-out of the BGSW AI Business from Robert Bosch India (BGSW) into a newly formed 50/50 Joint Venture (JV) with a Buyer entity (TBC). The carve-out will separate AI IT infrastructure, applications, and operations from BGSW's core systems by 7 Dec 2026 (Day 1 GoLive).

Sponsor Customer (Buyer): TBC – not yet disclosed. To be formally engaged post-project kickoff.

Sponsor Contractor (Seller): Robert Bosch India (BGSW) as the carver entity. Bosch BD/MIL provides programme management and guidance.

Governance Lead: Bosch BD/MIL (PMO) | Carver Lead: BGSW CIO

Scope & Model

In Scope

  • ~20 AI applications (Business and Analytics)
  • <100 IT users across 3 India sites
  • Bangalore IT Hub (primary)
  • Hyderabad IT Team (secondary)
  • Pune IT Team (tertiary)
  • IT Infrastructure & networNUMBERing (3 sites)
  • M365 and Azure tenant (JV domain)
  • Security and IAM for AI entity
  • TSA from BGSW (residual scope only; ~3-4 months post-GoLive)

Out of Scope

  • BGSW core applications (non-AI)
  • Bosch shared corporate services (retained by Bosch)
  • ERP separation (no SAP complexity)
  • Manufacturing/OT systems
  • Non-India geographies
  • Buyer-specific applications (unknown until buyer identified)
Carve-Out Model: Combination (Carver + JV)
Carver: BGSW operates the AI business IT as the carver entity during separation and transitions to JV ownership by Day 1. JV Structure: 50/50 JV with unknown buyer; JV assumes full IT independence post-TSA exit (target Mar 2027). TSA Scope: Minimal residual BGSW services (vs. full TSA in Stand Alone models); 3-month exit window post-GoLive.

Key Success Criteria

  • Day 1 GoLive (7 Dec 2026): All <100 IT users and ~20 AI applications fully operational in JV independent environment.
  • JV IT Independence: JV manages all AI IT operations with minimal BGSW TSA support; no production blockers on BGSW dependence.
  • Data Separation: 100% of AI business data migrated cleanly to JV environment; zero data loss or compliance breach.
  • User Acceptance: UAT sign-off by 20 Nov 2026; Hypercare Period (30 days post-GoLive) closes without critical open issues.
  • TSA Exit: All BGSW residual TSA services exited by 23 Jan 2027; no extensions required.
  • Budget Compliance: Project delivers within EUR 1.05M labour budget (+10% contingency); CAPEX TBC within board approval.

Key Risks & Constraints

Top Risks (Risk Rating 12+)

  • BGSW/JV Availability (Rating 16): Carve-out execution parallel to AI business operations; split focus risk. Mitigation: Dedicated carve-out lead; explicit time allocation.
  • JV Legal Registration (Rating 15): MCA (India) bureaucratic delays beyond QG1 target. Mitigation: External India counsel; parallel BGSW-only setup path if needed.
  • Budget Growth (Rating 12): India cloud/connectivity costs underestimated. Mitigation: Detailed RFQ by May 2026; price lock by QG1.
  • TSA Exit Delays (Rating 10): Service separation incomplete; JV not ready by Mar 2027. Mitigation: TSA catalogue lock at QG2; weekly governance reviews.
  • Resource Attrition (Rating 12): BGSW IT team uncertainty or burnout during carve-out. Mitigation: Early comms; retention plan; contractor backup.

Constraints

  • Hard GoLive Date: 7 Dec 2026 is tied to legal separation agreement. No post-ponement is a schedule constraint.
  • Buyer TBC: Buyer identity not yet disclosed; JV scope may shift once buyer is known. Change control gate at QG2.
  • India Regulatory: RBIN, tax registration, data residency approval timelines (3-6 months) are out-of-project control. Planned 12+ week lead time required.
  • No ERP: Simplifies complexity vs AlphaX but removes familiar migration templates. Custom approach required.

Assumptions

  • Buyer entity will be disclosed and engaged by 30 Jun 2026 (QG1) at the latest.
  • BGSW management commits full-time carve-out lead to project (no rotation mid-project).
  • Budget baseline of EUR 1.05M (labour) + TBC CAPEX approved by Steering Committee before QG1.
  • No major competing IT initiatives in BGSW during May–Dec 2026 that would draw resources from carve-out.
  • India regulatory approvals (RBIN, tax, data residency) can be filed immediately and will complete within 12 weeks.
  • <100 users and ~20 applications scope remains stable; additions post-QG1 follow change control.
