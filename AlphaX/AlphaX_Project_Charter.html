📋 1. Executive Summary

Project AlphaX is the IT workstream delivering the full technological separation of the Bosch Battery Division into a standalone, independently operating NewCo entity. Unlike integration-model carve-outs, AlphaX has no Merger Zone concept — all Battery IT systems, data, and infrastructure must be cleanly separated from the Bosch corporate environment and stood up independently within NewCo's own IT landscape.

Bosch acts as Sponsor Contractor: it currently owns and operates all Battery IT and will maintain Transitional Service Agreement (TSA) support for NewCo until handover completion in Q4 2027. NewCo is the Sponsor Customer: the Battery Division's new operating entity, whose permanent buyer is still in sourcing; NewCo will operate as an independent legal entity from Day 1.

The total programme spans 19 months (June 2026 – December 2027) across ~35 Battery sites and ~3,000 users. This is an aggressive timeline requiring strict scope governance, early vendor contracting, and parallel workstream execution from kick-off.

🗺️ 5. Delivery Approach and Model

Stand Alone model — the Battery Division becomes fully IT-independent rather than merging into a buyer's existing environment. This is the highest-complexity carve-out model because NewCo must build an entirely new IT stack from scratch, while Bosch TSA covers continuity in the interim.

No Merger Zone: there is no intermediate hybrid environment. Battery IT systems are either migrated to the NewCo environment at cutover or remain under TSA until individually separated. This requires a highly disciplined wave plan and strict cutover sequencing.

Key approach principles:

PrincipleApplication for AlphaX
"First make it work, then make it better"Day 1 readiness is the goal; optimise NewCo IT landscape post-separation
TSA = transition bridge, not permanent stateAll TSA services must have defined exit criteria; no open-ended TSA lines
Big Bang vs staggeredStaggered wave migration by site region; SAP and M365 in coordinated cutover
Frozen Zone from SigningNo production changes to Battery IT from 15 Jun 2026 without SteerCo approval
Point of No ReturnSAP shell copy execution and WAN cutover per site are Points of No Return; require go/no-go gate
