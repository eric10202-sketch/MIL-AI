Project Overview

Project Hamburger is the IT carve-out of Robert Bosch GmbH's Solar Energy Business into a fully standalone, independently operating IT entity for hand-over to the Undisclosed Buyer (confidential for legal reasons). The programme runs over 14 months from 01 Apr 2026 to 30 May 2027, with a hard GoLive of 01 Dec 2026.

The programme operates on a Stand Alone model with no Merger Zone: every Solar IT system must be fully operational in an independent environment by Day 1. Bosch provides Transitional Service Agreement (TSA) coverage from GoLive until programme completion. All 17 global sites and 2,600 users must be migrated across 6 phases.

Critical path anchors: ERP/SAP separation (shared mandants / custom ABAP — Red risk), 17-site global WAN provisioning (8–12 week lead times), and Active Directory migration for 2,600 users. These must be sequenced from Phase 3 to meet GoLive.

Country-Specific Complexity Hotspots

🇩🇪 Germany (EMEA HQ)

Largest Bosch/Solar IT integration. SAP separation origin. Data residency under GDPR-DE strictest interpretation. Multiple Solar sites.

🇺🇸 United States

US data law requirements. Americas WAN hub site. Payroll IT separation. Employment law specific to US staff transfers.

🇨🇳 China

PIPL data residency law — strict local data storage required. Network firewall complexity. External connectivity limitations. Local legal entity review critical.

🇯🇵 Japan / APAC

APAC data sovereignty requirements. Japan-specific IT compliance (Act on Protection of Personal Information). Time-zone execution complexity for cutover.

🇦🇺 Australia

Australian Privacy Act. APAC WAN connectivity. Relatively smaller user base but full standalone IT required per entity.

🇳🇱 Netherlands / 🇫🇷 France

GDPR strictest in EU alongside Germany. Works council obligations for IT staff changes. Dutch and French labour law transfer rules apply.

🇧🇷 Brazil

LGPD (Brazilian GDPR equivalent). WAN lead times in Brazil notoriously long. Tax system complexity for IT asset transfer.

Remaining 10 EMEA Sites

Mix of Eastern Europe, Middle East, Africa. Varying data laws. Hub-spoke model from Germany. Regional Site Lead required per cluster.
