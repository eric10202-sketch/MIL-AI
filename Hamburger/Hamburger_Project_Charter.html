📋 1. Executive Summary

Project Hamburger is the IT workstream delivering the full technological separation of the Bosch Solar Energy Business into a standalone, independently operating legal entity ready to be handed over to the Undisclosed Buyer. The buyer identity is withheld for legal reasons; accordingly, the programme is designed as a fully self-sufficient Stand Alone model with no dependency on any buyer-side IT infrastructure — the Solar business will be operable as a fully independent IT entity as of Day 1 (01 Dec 2026).

Robert Bosch GmbH acts as Sponsor Contractor: it currently owns and operates all Solar Energy IT and will maintain Transitional Service Agreement (TSA) support for the Solar entity until handover completion by 30 May 2027. The Undisclosed Buyer is the Sponsor Customer and will receive a fully operational, standalone IT environment at handover.

The programme spans 14 months across 17 worldwide sites and 2,600 IT users in EMEA, Americas and APAC. Given the global footprint, the scale of ERP separation, and a fixed GoLive date of 01 Dec 2026, this is a high-complexity programme requiring start-to-finish strict governance.

🗺️ 5. Delivery Approach and Model

Stand Alone model: the Solar Energy business becomes fully IT-independent rather than merging into a buyer's existing environment. Because the buyer is undisclosed, all Solar IT must be designed as self-sufficient — independent of any buyer system, network, or identity.

No Merger Zone: Solar IT systems move directly from Bosch to the Solar standalone environment. There is no intermediate hybrid state. Bosch TSA covers continuity only for systems not yet migrated at Day 1 — all TSA services have contractual exit dates.

PrincipleApplication for Hamburger
Buyer-agnostic designAll Solar IT built as fully standalone; no buyer-side integration dependencies in baseline
"First make it work, then make it better"Day 1 readiness is the goal; optimise Solar IT landscape post-handover

Regional cutover waves17-site global cutover phased by region: EMEA first, then Americas, then APAC

ERP on critical pathSAP separation starts Phase 3 and gates GoLive; dedicated SAP migration partner required
Point of No ReturnWAN cutover per site and AD forest switch are Points of No Return; require explicit go/no-go at QG3
