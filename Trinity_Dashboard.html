Project Overview
Project Trinity is a full-scope IT carve-out separating the Johnson Controls International (JCI) business unit being acquired by Bosch. JCI will perform a complete IT separation across 180 global sites spanning Asia-Pacific, Americas, and EMEA, migrating all IT services into a temporary Merger Zone (standalone environment) before final integration into the Bosch landscape. JCI will continue to operate IT for Bosch under an 18-month TSA post Day-1 closing (01 Jul 2028), with full TSA exit and project closure targeted for 31 December 2029. The scope covers ~8,000 employees, ~6,000 client devices, and ~500 applications across a FRAME-comparable SAP/ERP landscape.

Key Risk Indicators (Pre-Project Assessment)
▲ High Risk
WAN Lead Time (4–6 Months)
Network orders for 180 sites must be placed no later than Phase 2 to avoid go-live delays. State-owned operators in AP/China are highest risk.
P:4 · I:5 · Rating: 20
▲ High Risk
SAP Migration Complexity
FRAME-comparable ERP landscape; shell copy + SIT1/SIT2/UAT cycle requires 9–12 months. Brazil ERP tax rules add significant complexity.
P:4 · I:5 · Rating: 20
▲ High Risk
TSA Dependency & Scope Creep
18-month TSA operated by JCI creates ongoing dependency. Undefined exit criteria or scope expansion risk cost overrun and delayed Bosch independence.
P:3 · I:5 · Rating: 15
◆ Medium Risk
IAM / IdM Implementation
6–9 month implementation window. Saviynt SC2 connector restricted to 10 days before closing in Bosch-governed environment — tight scheduling required.
P:3 · I:4 · Rating: 12
◆ Medium Risk
~500 App Categorisation
Accurate app inventory (LeanIX/CMDB) and wave assignment is prerequisite for all Phase 4 migrations. Undiscovered apps are a recurring carve-out risk.
P:4 · I:3 · Rating: 12
● Low Risk
Governance & Resourcing
Dual (Bosch + JCI) PMO structure with KPMG adds coordination overhead. SteerCo and RACI model must be established in Phase 1 to contain schedule risk.
